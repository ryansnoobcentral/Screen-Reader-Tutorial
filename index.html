Screen Reader Tutorial for MacBooks VoiceOver Using Google Chrome

This tutorial is directed at users who are in possession of a MacBook(macOS 13 Ventura), Google Chrome browser (version 109.0.5414.120), and macOS built in VoiceOver.

Easy to Navigate Website Example: Google

To start this tutorial, we will introduce you to one of the most easily accessible websites, Google. Once you've navigated your way to Google's home page, you will be immediately put within Google's search bar. In the case that you were not, you will have to TAB about 6 times, which will then put you in the search box. Next, you can directly type your search in without further manipulation besides hitting enter to command Google to populate your search. Notice that when your search page populates, google will have the option to allow you to skip to the beginning of your search results directly. From here, you will need to use the key combination of control - option - space, which is used to click a link. Finally, once situated within the first search result. You then can navigate your way through the searches by hitting TAB. If, for some reason, you would like to read further about a particular search result; you can hit the key combination of control - option - shift - arrow down(to get to the next level of text) or arrow up(to get back to the top level of text). Suppose you've found a result matching your expectations. You can then hit the same key combination of control - option - space to enter the search results link.

This concludes our easy to navigate website example.

Hard to Navigate Website Example: Craigslist

Now, we will introduce to you one of the more difficult websites to navigate using macOS's VoiceOver, Craigslist. The company Craigslist has been around since 1995 and the functionality of their website sure does feel like its stuck there. In this tutorial, you will come to realize how inefficient navigating the Craigslist website will be under the direction of VoiceOver.

Before you begin, make sure to have your VoiceOver turned on, this can be accessed under System Preferences/Accessibility/VoiceOver/Enable VoiceOver

  • Step 1: Open craigslist.com
    1. Press Tab until you reach the craigslist link
    2. Press Control-Option-Space to open the link
  • Step 2: Navigate to the Anchorage, Alaska page - See Failure
    1. Tab/Shift-Tab to find the US link
    2. Control-Option-Space to open US link
    3. Tab 9 or 10 times (varies is you start on US or Auburn)
    4. Control-Option-Space to open Anchorage
  • Step 3: Open antiques page
    1. Tab until you reach heading level 3 link "for sale"
    2. Tab once
    3. Control-Option-Space
  • Step 4: Open someone's post
    1. Tab until you reach a posting
    2. Control-Option-Space to open post
    3. Tab/Shift-Tab to navigate through post
    4. Right and Left used to switch between photos

Failure

Craigslist fails using VoiceOver because it neglects reading the state names which categorize the cities. This is a problem because when navigating through the US states, if you could not see which state you are in, the only way to figure it out would be using the cities, but that is challenging and not everyone knows exactly which cities are where.
